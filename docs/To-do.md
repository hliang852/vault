## 2026-07-07 -- community-detection tuning and redundancy review

- **k-clique degeneracy root-caused: both a threshold problem and inherent to the graph's shape.** Measured the full 1,891-pair score distribution: 79.7% of all pairs already cleared the old `MIN_SCORE=1.5`, and 31.6% cleared it from just 6 near-universal features alone (before any genuinely distinguishing fact was shared) -- confirming the threshold was the dominant cause. But a grid search up to threshold=9.0 also showed k-clique percolation on this corpus always produces one dominant "generic-deal" community plus a few small (3-6 node) satellite communities, even at very high thresholds -- that split is real (most of the 62 cases share fairly ordinary combinations of features; only a handful share truly rare, distinctive combinations), not just a parameter artifact. **Decision: added a new `COMMUNITY_MIN_SCORE=7.0` constant (separate from the viewer's `MIN_SCORE=1.5`, which is unchanged), `K_CLIQUE=3`.** Post-fix (and after the redundancy consolidation below) this produces 5 communities, sizes [16, 3, 3, 3, 3], 26/62 nodes covered -- a meaningfully better, more balanced split than the 1-giant-community degenerate result, though still leaves 36 nodes with no "tight" cluster (itself informative -- most cases are comparatively ordinary rather than belonging to a small distinctive archetype). Revisit `COMMUNITY_MIN_SCORE`/`K_CLIQUE` again once Part 3 actually builds a UI consuming `community_ids`, since "what looks good in a chart" may differ from what looked good in this text-only review.
- **Redundancy review: lift-over-independence separated real correlation from base-rate artifacts.** Computed observed-vs-expected-under-independence ("lift") for all 9 previously-flagged high-Jaccard pairs, not just raw Jaccard -- this matters because two features that are each individually common will show high Jaccard overlap even with zero real correlation. Result: only 3 of 9 pairs showed genuine above-base-rate correlation (`structure_has_TOB`x`has_JFTC` 1.54x, `has_JFTC`x`has_DOJ_FTC_HSR` 1.41x, `structure_has_TOB`x`has_DOJ_FTC_HSR` 1.37x); the other 6 (including the highest raw-Jaccard pair, `timeline_post_meti_2023_guideline`x`timeline_post_tse_reform_2023` at 0.93) were lift ~1.0-1.09x -- almost entirely base-rate, not real redundancy.
  - **Consolidated `has_JFTC` + `has_DOJ_FTC_HSR`** (genuine correlation, `has_JFTC`'s True set was an exact subset of `has_DOJ_FTC_HSR`'s in this corpus) into a new `has_dual_antitrust_review` column/feature (both true, 39/62, weight 0.5, replacing the separately-scored 0.1+1.0). `structure_has_TOB` kept separate and unchanged (0.5) -- it's a deal-structure fact, not a regulatory one, even though it correlates with the antitrust flags.
  - **Consolidated `timeline_post_meti_2023_guideline` + `timeline_post_tse_reform_2023`** (base-rate artifact, not genuine redundancy, but consolidated anyway per a maintainer caution call) into a new `timeline_post_2023_reforms` column/feature (both true, 53/62, weight 0.1). `timeline_post_fiea_2026_amendment` stays separate (different regime, different date, currently inert anyway).
  - Re-ran the Jaccard check on the final feature set: down to 2 remaining pairs above 0.6 (`structure_has_TOB`x`has_dual_antitrust_review` 0.80, kept deliberately; `toehold_present_flag`x`timeline_post_2023_reforms` 0.67, already assessed as base-rate/lift~1.0, no further action).
  - `has_JFTC`, `has_DOJ_FTC_HSR`, `timeline_post_meti_2023_guideline`, `timeline_post_tse_reform_2023` remain in `data/Japan.csv` as individual descriptive columns -- only their contribution to `pair_score` was consolidated, not the underlying data.
